Our Team

Gathering Materials

First, we gathered all of our needed materials. Magnets, bearings, copper and silicone wires, diodes, bolts and screws were obtained online from Amazon and Home Depot. The frame and additional parts were 3D printed using a design by Tom Stanton on Printables.

Magnets

The magnets were positioned with alternating poles to optimize the magnetic field. As the hand crank is turned, the magnets interact with the coils to induce an electric current. Each magnet was carefully secured to maintain steady rotation and reliable energy generation.

Coils

The two halves of each 3D-printed coil were glued together, and zinc-coated copper wire was wrapped around all eight coils. They were placed in the frame to interact with the magnets and generate electricity when the hand crank is turned.

Wiring

The wires and diodes were soldered together, ensuring proper connections. The zinc coating on the wires was removed with heat at the contact points to allow conductivity, and the setup was tested by spinning the coils with a voltmeter.

In Action

All components were super glued together and the final product was put to the test.
