Our Team

Gathering Materials

We first collected all the materials needed for the project. Wood, nails, screws, and washers were picked up from Home Depot, and we found a used couch was found on the side of the street. The wheels, arduino kit, and soldering kit were ordered online from Amazon. Additional tools such as our saw and power drill were obtained from our garage.

Cutting Wood

The frame of the cart was constructed by cutting the 2x4 wood planks to the right sizes with a dull saw we found.

Frame and wheels

The pieces of wood were drilled together with four nails on each end to form a stable base. The four non-motorized wheels were drilled to the corners of the frame with four nails each, allowing the frame to be secure and move freely in any direction.

Soldering

The wheels with built-in motors were wired by soldering connections and heat shrinking to ensure proper power flow. Attention was paid to make sure the wiring was secure to ensure the wheels would drive properly and power the entire build.

Coding

For the code to control our build, we programmed the motorized wheels using Arduino and C++. We uploaded the code to the Arduino board and connected it to our motorized wheels in order to control movement with our joystick.

Wiring

We added the wiring board on our frame, connecting our motorized wheels, joystick, batteries and arduino all together to allow the couch to move from the joystick's control alone.

In Action

All components were assembled together, the couch was mounted on the wooden frame and the cart was tested to confirm that everything functioned smoothly
